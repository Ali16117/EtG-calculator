What Does 100 ng/mL Mean?

Introduction

Receiving a result of 100 ng/mL on an EtG test is not uncommon, especially in scenarios of very light alcohol consumption. Let's understand what this level indicates.

What is EtG?

EtG, short for ethyl glucuronide, is a direct metabolite of alcohol created by the liver. The detection of EtG in urine is a reliable indicator of recent alcohol intake.

Interpreting 100 ng/mL

A result of 100 ng/mL in an EtG test typically indicates low levels of alcohol exposure. This may point to:

  • Very Light Drinking: This level often corresponds with very light or infrequent alcohol consumption.
  • Recent Abstinence: It could also suggest that a longer period has elapsed since last consuming alcohol.

What It Doesn't Tell:

It's important to remember that a 100 ng/mL result doesn't specify the amount of alcohol consumed or the exact timing of consumption. It is not a direct indicator of intoxication.

Factors Influencing Results:

EtG levels can be influenced by individual factors such as metabolic rate, body composition, and hydration status.

Conclusion

While a 100 ng/mL EtG test result indicates a low level of alcohol exposure, context and individual factors play a crucial role in interpretation. Discussing the results with a healthcare professional can provide more personalized insights.
