What Does 250 ng/mL Mean?

Introduction

A result of 250 ng/mL in an EtG test indicates a level that suggests mild alcohol consumption or potential exposure to alcohol-containing products.

What is EtG?

EtG, or ethyl glucuronide, is a metabolite produced by the liver when it processes alcohol. It is used as a biomarker to detect recent alcohol intake.

Interpreting 250 ng/mL

An EtG level of 250 ng/mL can suggest:

  • Mild Alcohol Consumption: This level may be associated with low-level alcohol consumption.
  • Exposure to Alcohol-Containing Products: Use of products containing alcohol, such as certain mouthwashes or hand sanitizers, could also result in this level.

What It Doesn't Tell:

It's important to note that 250 ng/mL does not specify the quantity of alcohol consumed or the exact timing of its consumption. This level is not a definitive measure of intoxication.

Factors Influencing Results:

Several factors can affect EtG levels, including individual metabolic differences, body composition, and recent use of alcohol-containing products.

Conclusion

A 250 ng/mL EtG test result suggests mild alcohol exposure, but the interpretation should consider personal habits and potential exposure to alcohol in products. Discussing these results with a healthcare provider is recommended for accurate assessment and guidance.
