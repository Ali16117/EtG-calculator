What Does 350 ng/mL Mean?

Introduction

A result of 350 ng/mL in an EtG test typically indicates an increased likelihood of alcohol use in the recent past.

What is EtG?

EtG, short for ethyl glucuronide, is a metabolite that's produced when the body metabolizes alcohol. The presence of EtG in urine is a reliable marker for alcohol ingestion.

Interpreting 350 ng/mL

At a level of 350 ng/mL, the EtG test suggests recent alcohol consumption. This level is associated with:

  • Recent Alcohol Use: This level can be indicative of recent drinking, possibly within the last day or two.
  • Higher Alcohol Consumption: It might also suggest a higher quantity of alcohol consumption compared to lower EtG levels.

What It Doesn't Tell:

It's essential to note that a 350 ng/mL result doesn't quantify the exact amount of alcohol consumed or determine the exact time of drinking. It is not a direct measure of intoxication levels.

Factors Influencing Results:

The EtG level can be influenced by individual factors such as metabolism, body weight, and overall health status, among others.

Conclusion

A 350 ng/mL result in an EtG test is indicative of recent alcohol consumption. The interpretation of this result should be done considering the individual's drinking habits and health condition. A discussion with a healthcare provider is recommended for a detailed assessment and guidance.
