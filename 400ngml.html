What Does 400 ng/mL Mean?

Introduction

An EtG test result of 400 ng/mL falls into a moderate to significant range, which often suggests recent and moderate alcohol intake.

What is EtG?

EtG, or ethyl glucuronide, is a metabolite created when the liver processes alcohol. It's a reliable indicator of alcohol consumption.

Interpreting 400 ng/mL

At a level of 400 ng/mL, an EtG test implies:

  • Moderate to Significant Alcohol Use: This level typically indicates recent consumption of a moderate amount of alcohol.
  • Potential Frequent Drinking: It may also reflect frequent alcohol use over a period of time.

What It Doesn't Tell:

Importantly, a 400 ng/mL result doesn't specify the exact amount of alcohol consumed or the specific timing of drinking. It's not a direct indicator of intoxication or impairment level.

Factors Influencing Results:

EtG levels can be influenced by several factors, including individual metabolism, the type of alcoholic beverage consumed, and overall health status.

Conclusion

A result of 400 ng/mL in an EtG test is significant and suggests moderate alcohol consumption. Understanding the full context, including drinking habits and health condition, is key to interpreting this result. Consulting with a healthcare provider is advisable for a comprehensive assessment and guidance.
