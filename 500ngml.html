What Does 500 ng/mL Mean?

Introduction

An EtG test result of 500 ng/mL is often considered indicative of likely moderate drinking. This level may be seen after moderate alcohol consumption or as residual levels from heavy drinking a few days prior.

What is EtG?

EtG, or ethyl glucuronide, is a byproduct of alcohol metabolism in the liver. Its detection in urine is a reliable indicator of recent alcohol intake.

Interpreting 500 ng/mL

At a level of 500 ng/mL, the EtG test typically suggests:

  • Moderate Alcohol Consumption: This level can indicate moderate drinking.
  • Recent Drinking: It may also suggest that alcohol was consumed within the last few days, especially if it was in larger amounts.

What It Doesn't Tell:

It's crucial to understand that a 500 ng/mL result does not determine the exact quantity of alcohol consumed, nor does it indicate the specific time of drinking. It's not a direct measure of intoxication or impairment.

Factors Influencing Results:

The EtG level can be influenced by several factors, including individual metabolism, the type and amount of alcoholic beverage consumed, and overall health conditions.

Conclusion

A 500 ng/mL EtG test result suggests moderate alcohol exposure. Interpretation of this result should take into account personal drinking patterns and health conditions. Consulting with a healthcare professional is recommended for a comprehensive understanding and appropriate guidance.
