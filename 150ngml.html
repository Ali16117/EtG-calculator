What Does 150 ng/mL Mean?

Introduction

An EtG test result of 150 ng/mL is a relatively low level, but it's higher than the minimal detection threshold. This level can provide insights into recent alcohol use.

What is EtG?

EtG, or ethyl glucuronide, is a metabolite that forms when the body processes alcohol. Its presence in urine is a reliable indicator of alcohol consumption in the recent past.

Interpreting 150 ng/mL

At a level of 150 ng/mL, the EtG test suggests a slight increase in alcohol exposure. This may indicate:

  • Recent Light Drinking: This level can be associated with recent, but not heavy, alcohol consumption.
  • Decreasing Levels: It could also indicate that alcohol was consumed a few days ago, and the levels are declining.

What It Doesn't Tell:

A result of 150 ng/mL does not specify the exact amount of alcohol consumed or the precise time of drinking. It is not a direct measure of intoxication or impairment.

Factors Influencing Results:

EtG levels can be affected by individual factors like the body's metabolic rate, hydration, and overall health condition.

Conclusion

A 150 ng/mL EtG test result is indicative of some level of alcohol exposure. Understanding the context and individual factors is key to interpreting this result accurately. Consultation with a healthcare provider is advised for a comprehensive assessment.
